# Launch Strategy + First 100 Users Playbook

Sin usuarios en día 1, el código más hermoso es un hobby. Este building block es la estrategia de cómo conseguir las primeras 100 personas que paguen o usen activamente.

---

## Pre-Launch: 4 Semanas Antes

### Week 1: Pre-Game Setup

**Build your launching pad:**
```
☐ Landing page (copy + email signup)
☐ Product Hunt readiness (account created, graphics ready)
☐ Twitter/X bio updated (clear value prop)
☐ Email list building started (run 1-2 weeks)
☐ Influencer/press list (50 people who might care)
```

**Landing page essentials:**
```
Hero: One-line value prop + CTA "Get early access"
Subheader: The problem in 1 sentence
3 sections: Problem / Solution / Why You
Social proof: (leave empty for now, add testimonials after beta)
CTA: Email signup with early access incentive
```

**Email list building (target: 500 emails):**
- Micro-Twitter threads (5-7 tweets) about the problem
- Reply to others solving similar problems
- Comment on Product Hunt discussions
- Mention in Slack communities (where your users are)

---

### Week 2: Beta Recruitment

**Get 20 beta testers (pay them or reward them):**

Option A: Free beta + later discount
```
"Join the beta. Free for 3 months, then $29/month. 
Early users get 50% lifetime discount."
```

Option B: Paid beta
```
"$9 for 3 months of beta access (limited spots, then $29/month)"
→ Filters for serious users
```

**Where to recruit:**
```
1. Your email list (500 people → aim for 5-10% conversion = 25-50 beta users)
2. Twitter replies (post: "looking for 20 beta testers, reply here")
3. Relevant Slack/Discord communities
4. ProductHunt early access list
5. Your personal network (friends who actually want this)
```

**Onboarding for beta testers:**
```
Email 1: Welcome + access link + 3 things to try
Email 2 (day 3): "How's it going? Send feedback anytime"
Email 3 (day 7): Specific feedback survey (4 questions max)
Email 4 (day 14): "Found a bug? Feature request?" + community link
```

---

### Week 3: Iteration Based on Beta Feedback

**Monitor:** 
```
- Who uses it? (personas match?)
- How often? (usage patterns)
- What breaks? (bugs/crashes)
- What's confusing? (UX issues)
- What's missing? (feature requests)
```

**Fix top 3 issues from beta feedback.** Don't gold-plate. Move fast.

---

### Week 4: Launch Preparation

**48 hours before launch:**
```
☐ Double-check: login works, payments work, email works
☐ Press email list ready (50-100 people)
☐ ProductHunt post pre-written and scheduled
☐ Twitter launch thread pre-written (5-7 tweets)
☐ 1 press/influencer contacted individually (not mass email)
☐ Analytics set up (PostHog, Google Analytics)
☐ Status page / downtime protocol documented
```

**Press email template (50 words max):**
```
Subject: [Your name] just shipped [Product] — [1-line value prop]

Hi [Name],

I just launched [Product]. It [solves X problem] in [way they care about].

You might find it useful for [specific angle for them].

Link: [link]

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Twitter launch thread:**
```
Tweet 1: Hook — the problem in 1 tweet
Tweet 2: The pain — concrete example
Tweet 3: Existing solutions suck because...
Tweet 4: Our solution does X/Y/Z
Tweet 5: We just launched. Link in next tweet.
Tweet 6: Link + "replies = feature requests"
Tweet 7: Bonus (metric, testimonial, or behind-the-scenes)
```

## Launch Day Strategy

### The 24-Hour Blitz

**Morning (6-12 hours before):**
```
☐ ProductHunt post goes LIVE at exact time
☐ Email your 500-person list ("It's live!")
☐ Post on Twitter (thread from above)
☐ Post in relevant communities (HN, Reddit, Slack)
☐ Reach out to 20 influencers individually ("Hey, just launched!")
```

**Afternoon (6 hours after launch):**
```
☐ Check ProductHunt comments every 30 min
☐ Respond to EVERY comment within 1 hour
☐ Fix any critical bugs immediately
☐ Retweet/engage with mentions
```

**Evening:**
```
☐ Send email to anyone who commented
☐ Start calling beta testers: "You're on the list of first 50, wanna try the real thing?"
☐ Identify 5 people who can help spread word
```

## Post-Launch: Days 2-7

### Week 1: Momentum

**Daily for 7 days:**
```
☐ Email everyone who signed up (1 email/day max)
  Day 1: "It's live" + link
  Day 2: "5 features you probably missed"
  Day 3: Customer story (or your own)
  Day 4: FAQ + common objections answered
  Day 5: "We're almost at 50 users, join them"
  Day 6: Discount/limited offer ("$19/mo for first 100 users")
  Day 7: Closing statement + link
```

**Growth tactics:**
```
1. ProductHunt: respond to comments, build momentum
2. Twitter: daily tweets about usage, feature, or user stories
3. Reddit: answer in relevant communities (don't spam)
4. Communities: reply helpfully in Slack/Discord
5. Referral: "Refer a friend, get X" (works 20% of the time)
```

**Metrics to track:**
```
- Signups/day (target: 10-20)
- Conversion to paying (target: 5-10%)
- Free trial usage (% active daily)
- Churn (if month-long trial, track week 1 vs 4)
- NPS (send 1-question survey day 7: "rate 0-10")
```

## Weeks 2-4: First 100 Users

### Strategies by User Type

**Strategy A: Community Building (For Social/Marketplace/Creator tools)**
```
Where: Twitter, Reddit, Product Hunt, indie hacker communities
How: Daily engagement, authentic answers, help before selling
Timeline: Slow (2-3 months to 100 users)
Cost: Your time

Best for: Bootstrapped, founder who loves community
```

**Strategy B: Paid Acquisition (For Clear ROI SaaS)**
```
Where: Google Ads, Twitter Ads, Reddit Ads
Budget: $500-2000 test budget
Cost per signup: $5-20
Cost per customer: $50-200

Breakeven: $X customer LTV > $X CAC
Best for: Founder with budget, clear value prop
```

**Strategy C: Partnership/Influencer (For Everyone)**
```
Who: 10-20 people with relevant audiences
Pitch: "Use my app, get X" (discount / affiliate)
Timeline: Fast (2-3 weeks to 50 users)
Cost: Discounts + time

Best for: Founder with existing networks or connections
```

**Strategy D: Founder-Led Sales (For B2B SaaS)**
```
Method: You email 100 potential customers, 20% reply, 10% trial, 5% buy = 5 customers
Timeline: Slow (3-6 months)
Cost: Your time

Best for: B2B where each customer is valuable
```

### The First 100 Checklist

```
☐ 10+ ProductHunt upvotes (day 1 = success)
☐ 50 email signups
☐ 20 trial signups
☐ 10 paying customers
☐ 5 reviews / testimonials
☐ 1000 Twitter impressions (cumulative)
☐ 1 press mention (doesn't have to be major)
☐ Clear feedback on: what works / what's broken / what's missing
```

## Weeks 5-12: Getting to 100 Customers

### Growth Loop You Should Repeat

```
Week 1: Launch, 10 customers
Week 2: Iteration based on feedback, 15 customers (total 25)
Week 3: Targeted outreach + paid ads, 20 customers (total 45)
Week 4: Press coverage + influencer, 20 customers (total 65)
Week 5-8: Organic growth + paid, 10/week = 40 (total 105)
```

### Success Signals

✅ 5+ customers say "this changed my [work/life]"
✅ Week-over-week growth 20%+
✅ NPS > 50
✅ Churn < 5% monthly
✅ Repeat customers / enthusiasts

### Failure Signals

🚩 After week 4, still < 20 customers
🚩 Nobody uses it 2+ times
🚩 All users come from direct you asking (no organic)
🚩 NPS < 30
🚩 Churn 10%+ after first month

If 2+ red flags → pause, gather feedback, iterate or pivot.

## Launch Strategy Template (By Archetype)

### SaaS (B2B)
- ProductHunt + Twitter + relevant communities
- Founder-led sales to 20 prospects
- Timeline: 12 weeks to 100 customers

### SaaS (B2C)
- ProductHunt + Twitter + TikTok/YouTube
- Referral rewards (viral mechanics)
- Organic community growth
- Timeline: 8 weeks to 100 users (maybe 10-20 paying)

### Marketplace
- Two-sided supply/demand problem
- First: recruit supply (sellers/providers)
- Then: recruit demand (buyers/customers)
- Timeline: 16 weeks to 100 users

### AI Product
- ProductHunt is HUGE for AI launches
- Technical communities (Hacker News, Reddit r/MachineLearning)
- Educational content (Twitter threads about AI)
- Timeline: 6-10 weeks to 100 users
